Our Family

Rugby Football

Walter and Emma West had nine children, six of them were boys, three of them were very successful on the rugby field. Herbert was six years older than Thomas, and eighteen years older than Reginald. All the boys played at the same position, scrum-half, but Tom also played at outside-half (alongside Herbert in a few first team games for Bath) and, for the major part of his career, at wing three-quarter. Herbert sustained an eye injury, which probably limited his career, but Thomas played ninety games for Bath, including a season as captain of the club before moving to Lancashire to play for Rochdale Hornets, where players received payment for playing.

The next generation, grandsons of Walter and Emma, were also involved in the local rugby clubs. Cyril (Bud) West was a very successful full-back, though his playing career for Bath was interrupted by military service in the second world war. Charles (Joe) Kent, who had been at school with Bud, and Ken Sherriff, who married Phyllis West, also played for the club.

Until 1995, rugby union was an exclusively amateur game, so that players for local teams would be men living locally to the clubs and, most often, men who had learned the game together at local schools. This made the sport, and its clubs, a particularly strong element of many local communities.
